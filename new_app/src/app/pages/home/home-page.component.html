The top 1000 of medieval castles

Editorial ranking

Across Europe and Asia, more than 10,000 medieval castles can still be found. Topcastles.com selected the most beautiful and interesting castles and ranked them in a top 1000 list. Every square picture of a castle on this site links to the information page of that castle. The methodology behind of this list is explained in the menu background.

Editor's note The ranking weighs architectural significance, state of preservation, historical importance, and accessibility. Castles that survive as ruins can still rank very highly if their historical context is exceptional.

Visitor rating

Between 2004 and 2021, 17,300 visitors of topcastles.com and topkastelen.nl* submitted 63,800 ratings for castles in the list. The visitor rating reflects real audience enthusiasm and is independent of the editorial ranking above — a castle ranked lower editorially may still appear here if it draws exceptional visitor interest. If you are interested in the most popular castles based on visitor ratings, you can click on the VISITORS* header in the table view of the top 1000 list.

Atlas notes

Brief editorial annotations on how medieval castle culture varies across the regions covered in the list.

  • Rhine & Moselle (Germany) The river valleys of the Rhine and Moselle hold the highest concentration of intact medieval fortifications in Europe. Many were rebuilt or heavily restored during the Romantic era, giving them the archetypal "fairy-tale" silhouette that defines the popular image of a castle.
  • Loire Valley (France) The Loire châteaux evolved from military strongholds into Renaissance pleasure residences. Their prominence in the list reflects both architectural ambition and exceptional preservation — several remain furnished and inhabited to this day.
  • British Isles English and Welsh castles are notable for their diversity of form: Norman mottes, Edwardian concentric rings, and Tudor tower houses appear across the list. Scottish tower houses occupy a distinct tradition, built for clan defence rather than feudal display.
