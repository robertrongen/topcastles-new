Plate I. Worldwide distribution of indexed entries, with editorial annotations.
  1. Rhine & Moselle: The river valleys of the Rhine and Moselle hold the highest concentration of intact medieval fortifications in Europe. Many were rebuilt during the Romantic era, giving them the archetypal "fairy-tale" silhouette.
  2. Loire Valley: The Loire châteaux evolved from military strongholds into Renaissance pleasure residences. Their prominence reflects both architectural ambition and exceptional preservation — several remain furnished and inhabited.
  3. British Isles: English and Welsh castles are notable for their diversity: Norman mottes, Edwardian concentric rings, and Tudor tower houses. Scottish tower houses occupy a distinct tradition, built for clan defence rather than feudal display.

About this list

Topcastles is an editorial ranking of the 1,000 most significant medieval castles across Europe and Asia — selected and scored for architectural quality, state of preservation, historical importance, and accessibility.

The list has been maintained since 2004. 63,800 visitor ratings were collected 2004–2021 from 17,300 respondents.
